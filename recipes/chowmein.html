Chowmein

What is Chowmein?

Chow mein is a Chinese dish made from stir-fried noodles with vegetables and sometimes meat or tofu. Over the centuries, variations of chǎomiàn were developed in many regions of China; there are several methods of frying the noodles and a range of toppings can be used.

Ingredients:

  1. ¼ cup oyster sauce
  2. 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  3. 3 cloves garlic, minced
  4. 2 teaspoons freshly grated ginger
  5. 1 teaspoon Sriracha
  6. 1 (16-ounce) package Hong Kong-style pan-fried noodles
  7. Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  8. 2 tablespoons canola oil
  9. 1 (3.5-ounce) package shiitake mushrooms
  10. 4 baby bok choy, coarsely chopped
  11. 1 cup mung bean sprouts, rinsed and drained

Steps:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together oyster sauce, soy sauce, garlic, ginger and Sriracha; set aside.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon canola oil in a large skillet or wok over medium high heat. Add noodles and cook, stirring constantly, until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes; set aside.
  3. Heat remaining 1 tablespoon canola oil in the skillet. Add mushrooms, and cook, stirring often, until tender, about 3-4 minutes. Stir in bok choy until just wilted, about 1 minute.
  4. Stir in noodles and oyster sauce mixture until well combined, about 2 minutes. Stir in bean sprouts.
  5. Serve and Enjoy!
